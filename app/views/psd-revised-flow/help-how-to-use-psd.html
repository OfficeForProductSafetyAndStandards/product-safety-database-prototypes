Help – How to operate the PSD

An introduction to using the Product Safety Database (PSD) online digital service.

In early 2023 – How cases work and are created has changed

Previously, when a case was created, basic information describing a product was automatically required and formed part of the PSD case. Instead, cases now include – or link to – product records. It is the product record which now records the basic information about the product.

A case and a product are now two separate
entities within the PSD.

Cases record information about allegations, evidence, and corrective actions. A product record describes the product and is included within a case.

The same product record can now be included within many different cases.

Overview

The two main areas within the PSD online service are the cases area and the products area. These are accessed using the main navigation menu (towards the top/start of each page).

In the cases area you can find cases you or your team have created or search the entire database of cases. A case is what ultimately reports unsafe, noncompliant and serious risk products to the OPSS and includes relevant information such as importer contact details, product batch numbers, test reports and corrective actions.

When a case is created, information and assets (such as accidents or incidents, reports and evidence images) can then be added directly to the case. But the basic information describing a product is added to a case by including a product record. A product record is what contains the basic information about the product.

The basic information about a product is added to a case by including a product record. In the PSD a case and a product record are two seperate entities.

In the products area you can find the product records which are included in your cases or search the entire database of product records. A product record only contains information about a product (such as its brand name and model name, barcode, and its catagorisation) and doesn't include case information. A single product record can be included within many cases.

Your PSD account will assign you to a team. You can find information about your team in the 'Your team' page accessed via the main navigation menu.

2. Create a case

The process of creating a case starts from a product record. A case needs to include at least one product record. This is made easy by first searching for a product within the products area.

  1. In the products area, search for a product using the
    'All products – Search' option*
  2. Open a product record to view it
  3. Select the 'Create a new case for this product' option
  4. Complete the form journey process

*If a product record describing the product does not already exist, you can create a new product record for the product.

When you have finished the process of creating a case, the product record will automatically be included within the new case. The new case can be found in the cases area, where you can continue adding further information and assets to the case.

When you create a case, you and your team are the case owners.

Once your case has been created you can add more product records to the same case. One example for why you might do this could involve a scenario where a manufacturer has created two or more versions of the same  product using different names and barcodes. These products would be represented by different product records, but you could add them all to the same case.

3. Find a case

In the cases area, the secondary navigation menu (on the left) provides three ways to find a case:

  1. 'Your cases' lists all your open cases
  2. 'Team cases' lists all your team's open cases
  3. 'All cases – Search' is where you can search all cases in the PSD

You can apply filters to search results to reduce how many product records are found in a search.

Select a case name in any list to open the main case page for the case.

4. Viewing case information

When you view a case the secondary navigation menu (on the left) presents the case information in logical groups and across different pages.

The secondary case navigation menu provides access to all the information that forms one case. These include, the main case page, the product(s), relevant businesses, case images and all the supporting information for the case, such as risk assessments and corrective actions.

On the products page for an individual case, you can find basic information about the product(s) included in the case, and see which cases within the PSD have also included the product record. To view all the cases relating to the same product record, select the 'Cases' tab.

5. Edit, add to, or close a case

If your team owns the case, or has been granted editing permission, the case pages provide 'edit', 'add', 'remove' or 'change' options throughout all the pages relating to the case.

You cannot edit or close a case if you do not have editing permission for the case.

On the main case page for an individual case, the team which owns the case can add other teams to the case and grant them editing permission. (Select 'Change' in the 'Teams added' row).

The main case page for an individual case also incorporates a convenient panel of options for adding information and assets to the case.

If your team owns the case, or you have editing permission, you can close a case by selecting the 'Close case' option on the main case page for an individual case. It is good practise to close your PSD case when you are satisfied you no longer need to add or change any information.

6. Add the OPSS Incident Management Team to a case

When you have finished adding information and assets to a case, you should add the OPSS Incident Management Team (IMT) to the case, if:

  • the product poses a high or serious risk
  • the product has been recalled
  • you need to notify overseas regulators

To add the Incident Management Team (IMT) to your case:

  1. Find the case
  2. Open the case to view the main case page
  3. In the 'Sharing and collaboration' section, select the 'Change' option in the 'Teams added to case' row
  4. On the 'Teams added to the case' page, select 'Add a team to the case'
  5. On the 'Add a team to the case' page, search for
    "OPSS Incident Management"
  6. Complete the form

It is good practise to close your PSD case when you are satisfied you no longer need to add or change any information.

7. Create a product record

A product record is what records the basic information about a product, such as its brand name, its model name, and its barcode. At least one product record should be included within a case (to represent the product). The same product record can be reused and included within many different cases.

Creating a product record is not the same as creating a case.

Before you create a new product record you should first check that a product record representing the product does not already exist within the Product Safety Database (PSD). You should avoid creating duplicates.

A counterfeit product and the real product should be represented by two different product records.

To create a product record:

  1. In the products area …
  2. Select the 'Create a new product record' option on the 'Your products', 'Team products' or 'All products – Search' page
  3. Complete the form journey process

When you have finished the process of creating a product record, it can be found by using the 'All products – Search' page. When it is included in one of your own cases or one of your team's cases, it will also appear in the 'Your products' or 'Team products' pages.

When you create a product record, your team has exclusive editing permission – and "owns" the product record – until your team closes all cases which have included the product record.

When you close a case, a unique copy of each product record included in the case is automatically created and permanently replaces the original versions for that individual case. This ensures the original product information in a case is protected after a case is closed.

8. Find a product record

In the products area, the secondary navigation menu (on the left) provides three ways to find a product record:

  1. 'Your products' lists all the product records included in your open cases
  2. 'Team products' lists all the product records included in your team's open cases
  3. 'All cases – Search' is where you can search all product records currently available in the PSD

You can also search for cases by using the product record details using the 'All cases – Search' page in the cases area. This can return all the cases which include, or have included, the product record; even if the original product record is no longer available.

9. Viewing product record information

When you view a product record the secondary navigation menu (on the left) presents the product information in logical groups.

The images added to a product record are images of the product and its packaging and are not case evidence images (which are added to cases).

The cases section of a product record lists all the cases which have included the product record.

Which team currently owns and has editing permission for the product record is also displayed. Any team can still include the product record within their own cases.

10. Edit a product record

Only one team has editing permission for a product record at any one time. This is to avoid conflicting edits which may compromise the product record.

If you have editing permission, select the 'Edit the product record' option on the product record page to make edits and changes in the form provided (and save your changes).

Some information in a product record – such as the product name – is permanent. This is to avoid product records changing beyond recognition and compromising any cases which include it. If the changes you require are significantly different then this may necessitate creating a new product record. (You should avoid editing a product record in order for it to represent a different product).

You can add and remove product records from your cases.

If you have additional information about a product or more product images, you can source the contact details for the team which currently owns the product record from a product record page.
