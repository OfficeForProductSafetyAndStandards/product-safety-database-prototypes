Help – How to operate the PSD

An introduction to using the Product Safety Database (PSD) online digital service.

In early 2023 – How cases work and are created was changed

Previously, when a case was created, basic information describing a product was automatically required and formed part of the PSD case. Instead, cases now include – or link to – product records. It is the product record which now records the basic information about the product.

A case and a product are now two separate
entities within the PSD.

Cases record information about allegations, evidence, and corrective actions. A product record describes the product and is included within a case.

The same product record can now be included within many different cases.

Overview

The two main areas within the PSD online service are the cases area and the products area. These are accessed using the main navigation menu (towards the top/start of each page).

In the cases area you can find cases you or your team have created or search the entire database of cases. A case is what ultimately reports unsafe, noncompliant and serious risk products to BEIS/OPSS and includes relevant information such as importer contact details, product batch numbers, test reports and corrective actions.

When a case is created, information and assets (such as accidents or incidents, reports and evidence images) can then be added directly to the case. But the basic information describing a product is added to a case by including a product record. A product record is what contains the basic information about the product.

The basic information about a product is added to a case by including a product record. In the PSD a case and a product record are two seperate entities.

In the products area you can find the product records which are included in your cases or search the entire database of product records. A product record only contains information about a product (such as its name and brandname, barcode, and its catagorisation) and doesn't include case information. A single product record can be included within many cases.

2. Create a case

The process of creating a case starts from a product record. A case needs to include – or link to – at least one product record. This is made easy by first searching for a product within the products area.

  1. In the products area, search for a product using the 'All products – Search' option*
  2. Open a product record to view it
  3. Select the 'Create a new case for this product' option
  4. Then follow the on-screen steps to create the case

*If a product record describing the product does not already exist, you can create a new product record for the product.

When you have finished the process of creating a case, the product record will automatically be included within the new case. The new case can be found in the cases area, where you can continue adding further information and assets to the case.

When you create a case, you and your team are the case owners.

3. Find a case

In the cases area, the secondary navigation menu (on the left) provides three ways to find a case:

  1. 'My cases', lists all your open cases
  2. 'Team cases', lists all your team's open cases
  3. 'All cases – Search', is where you can search all cases in the PSD

You can apply filters to search results to reduce how many product records are found in a search.

Select a case name in any list to open the main case page for the case.

4. Edit or close a case

When you view a case the secondary navigation menu (on the left) presents the case information in logical groups and across different pages. If you are a member of the team which owns the case, or you have been granted editing permission, the case pages provide 'edit', 'add', 'remove' or 'change' options throughout all the pages relating to the case.

You cannot edit or close a case if you do not have editing permission for the case.

On the main case page for a case, the team which owns the case can add other teams to the case and grant them editing permission. (Select 'Change' in the 'Teams added' row).

If your team owns the case, or you have editing permission, you can close a case by selecting the 'Close case' option on the main case page for a case.

5. Add the OPSS Incident Management Team to a case

When you have finished adding information and assets to a case and once the measures taken are completed, you should add the OPSS Incident Management Team (IMT) to the case, if:

  • the product poses a less than serious risk and has not been recalled*
  • the product has been recalled
  • the product poses a serious risk to the health and safety of consumers as determined by a risk assessment**

To add the Incident Management Team (IMT) to a case (if you have editing permission for the case):

  1. Find the case
  2. Open the case to view the main case page for the case
  3. In the 'Sharing and collaboration' section …
  4. Select the 'Change' option in the 'Teams added to case' data row
  5. On the next page ('Teams added to the case'), select 'Add a team to the case'
  6. On the 'Add a team to the case' page, search for
    "OPSS Incident Management"
  7. Complete the form

*And then close the case.

**If the product poses a serious risk, you must also set the OPSS Incident Management Team (IMT) as the case owner, which will ensure the recall can be added to the Government Recall Site and the OECD Global Recall Portal.

To set the OPSS Incident Management Team (IMT) as the case owner:

  1. Find the case
  2. Open the case to view the main case page for the case
  3. In the 'Sharing and collaboration' section …
  4. Select the 'Change' option in the 'Case owner' data row
  5. On the 'Change the case owner' page – assuming you have already added the OPSS Incident Management Team to the case – select 'OPSS Incident Management' as the case owner

About product records

A product record only records and includes information about a product. For example, its name, its barcode number and photographs of the product. It does not include information about safety concerns or incidents relating to the product because that is case specific information.

A product record can be included in more than one case. Teams from across the country can create cases from the same product record or add it to their cases.

A product record describing a counterfeit version of a product should be a different product record to one describing the genuine product.

While several product records might accidently describe the same product, we aim to have only one product record in the Product Safety Database describing a product.

You can create a new product record if one doesn't already exist for your product or if an existing one doesn't fit your needs.

About cases

A case records case specific information about an unsafe, or non-compliant, product. For example, case specific information might include the business address for the importer of the product or risk assessments about the product.

In the Product Safety Database (PSD), information about a case and a product are seperate. Information which describes a product is recorded as a product record and is available to be included as part of a case.

Product details and case information have been seperated to
create several benefits

  • With new cases and product records, you can now see which teams are also using the same product record in their PSD cases and if they are investigating the same product
  • With new cases and product records, you will now be able to see all investigations for a product and how they may have been resolved
  • There is no longer a need to input data about a product if a product record for the product already exists
  • The product records will have improved information and detail
  • By using the same product records across different teams and cases nationally, there is greater transparency, and you are able to share information or even avoid duplicating work
  • There will be fewer duplications of product entries in the database and searching for products will be more decisive
  • This will allow us to add more powerful features in the future.
