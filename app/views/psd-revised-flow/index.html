Create a case user-journey:

New HTML/design page templates

Here are the current page designs for revised 'Create a case' user-flow.

Case (pages):

  • Trading Standards - CASE page (Admin). Admin users and team members have all editing rights - if the product owner is on your team you have editing rights
    • Trading Standards - Case page (Non-Admin)
    • Trading Standards = Case page (different team)
    • Trading Standards Case page (Admin): after case creation
    • OPSS - CASE page (Allegation)
    • OPSS - CASE page (Allegation) - a team not added to the case, view. 2x classes removed from DL.
    • OPSS - CASE page (Allegation) - a team added to the case but without editing rights, view. 2x classes removed from DL + sentence at footer.
    • OPSS - CASE page (Enquiry)

In the left navigation: "Case admin" only appears for the case owner.

OPSS

In the Case source section is the statement: "Only teams added to the case can view case source contact details" which is shown if the OPSS user is in the team that owns this case

Products (pages):

  • Trading Standards - PRODUCTS page
    • Trading Standards - Products x2 page Showing two products linked to the one case. The text and missing edit link for a product record owned by another team and a product record without an owner.
  • Email the product record owner. The Bcc email will not include the product record owner's email address (no-replay@psd ?).
    • Email sent
  • Remove the product record from the case
    • Product removed
  • There are no products linked to this case (blank products page with statement - to design "…add a product to the case…)
  • Add a product to the case page. (Phase one version - simply ID entry).

The "Edit this product" link(s) only appear if the user is in the product record owner team or the product record has no owner.

Within the tabbed areas, the statements "To edit the [details/images/attachments] for this product go to the full [product name] product record page" only appear if the user is in the product record owner team or the product record has no owner. Alternatively, we serve this statement instead: "Only the product record owner ([Owner name]) can edit these [details/images/attachments]".
