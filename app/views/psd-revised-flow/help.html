Cases and product records – help

How a case is created has changed.

About cases

A case records case specific information about an unsafe, or non-compliant, product. Case specific information might include the business address for the importer of the product or risk assessments about the product.

In the Product Safety Database (PSD), information about a case and a product are seperate. Information which describes the product is recorded as a product record and you add the product record to your case.

Create a case and add a product

  1. Create your case –  this is now quicker with fewer questions
  2. Add the product to your case
    • Check to see the product does not already exist in the database - if it does, use its PSD reference number to add the product record to your case
    • If a suitable product record does not already exist, create a new product record and use its PSD reference number to add it to your case
  3. Continue to add any other information, such as businesses or test results, to your case at any time

– OR –

Find a product and create a case

  1. Find a product –  by searching the product records
  2. Create a new case for and from that product
    • When creating a case directly from a product record the product will automatically be added to the new case
    • If a suitable product record does not exist you can create one
  3. Continue to add any other information, such as businesses or test results, to your case at any time

About product records

A product record only records information about a product. For example, its name, its barcode number and photographs of the product. It does not include information about safety concerns or incidents relating to the product because that is case specific information.

A product record can be added to more than one case. Teams from across the country can create cases and add the same product record to their cases.

A product record describing a counterfeit version of a product should be a different product record to one describing the genuine product.

While several product records might accidently describe the same product, we aim to have only one product record in the Product Safety Database describing a product.

You can create your own product record if an existing one doesn't fit your needs or one doesn't already exist for your product

We have made the cases and products pages more useful and easier to use

  • There is now no need to input data about a product if a product record for your product already exists
  • The product records will have improved information and detail
  • You can now see if other teams are using the same product record in their investigations (and if they are investigating the same product)
  • You can contact other teams you now know to be investigating the same product. (Teams can add other teams to a case)
  • By using the same product records across different teams and cases nationally, there is greater transparency, and you are able to share information or even avoid duplicating work
  • There will be fewer duplications of product entries in the database and searching for products will be more decisive.
