This risk level approach is most suited to scenarios where a product has multiple independent hazards that involve risks with different injury severity levels. The risk associated with the product would, as normal, be derived from the hazard creating the greatest level of risk.

This method of combining risk probabilities is useful when a product has at least two independent hazards that involve the same level of injury severity. Adding the probabilities of harm presented by the two hazards would generate a greater overall probability of harm.
