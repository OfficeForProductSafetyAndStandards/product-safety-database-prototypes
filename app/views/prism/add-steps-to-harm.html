Creating a harm scenario requires consideration of how the identified hazard could cause harm to people. The scenario will normally contain between three or five steps to harm. The nature of probability is such that the more steps there are, in general the lower the probability will be. It is rare that more than five steps are necessary.

Step 1 description

Step 1 should cover the existence of a hazard which can foreseeably cause harm during the lifetime of the product. For example, the car seat is occupied by an infant while the vehicle is in use.

Step description

Step 2 should describe how the user (and/or other subjects) becomes exposed to risk. For example, the vehicle containing the seat/child is involved in a frontal collision of sufficient magnitude that the protection offered by the seat is necessary.
