What is the level of uncertainty associated with the risk assessment?

Every risk assessment that involves some degree of estimation will come with an associated degree of uncertainty; this is normal and does not in itself mean that the risk assessment is flawed.

Has sensitivity analysis been undertaken?

The implications of the uncertainty can be considered by undertaking a sensitivity analysis. For example, where there is uncertainty surrounding one or more of the probabilities within a harm scenario, then different probabilities can be applied (which can be higher, lower, or both, than the original figure/s used)) to fairly reflect the extent of the uncertainty.
